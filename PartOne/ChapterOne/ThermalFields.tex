\section{Thermal fields}

\subsection{Thermal fields}
A black body can be modeled as a cavity containing radiation at thermal equilibrium with its walls. 
The radiation is thus coupled to a heat bath and so is not a truly free field.
Assuming the coupling is weak, we can treat the field as if it were an isolated system that can be described as a microcanonical ensemble.

Lets consider a single-mode field in thermal equilibrium with the walls of cavity at temperature $T$. The probability $P_n$ that the mode is 
thermally xcited in the nth level is 
\begin{align*}
    P_n=\frac{\exp(-E_n/k_BT)}{\sum_n\exp(-E_n/k_BT)},\quad k_B=1.38\cdot10^{-23}\;J/K.
\end{align*}
We introduce the density operator for the thermal field:
\begin{align*}
    \rho_{Th}=\frac{\exp(-\hH/k_BT)}{\Tr{\exp(-\hH/k_BT)}},\quad\hH=\hbar\omega(\ha^\dagger\ha+\frac{1}{2}).
\end{align*}
